# The Biblical Meaning of Saints

The term "saint" often brings to mind images of stained-glass windows or
individuals who have performed miraculous deeds. Many people recognize saints as
holy figures who receive official honors from religious institutions. However,
the original use of the word in the Bible describes a different concept that
applied to all believers.

## The Dictionary Definition

Modern dictionaries often define a saint through the lens of ecclesiastical
tradition. This perspective focuses on official recognition and holy living.

> (the title given to) a person who has received an official honor from the
> Christian, especially the Roman Catholic, Church for having lived in a good
> and holy way. The names of saints are sometimes used to name places and
> buildings:
>
> * Saint Peter
> * St. Andrew's school
> * Saint Paul's Cathedral — [Cambridge Dictionary](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/saint)

This definition reflects the current understanding in many Christian
denominations. It suggests that a saint is someone who has reached a specific
level of virtue recognized by church leadership.

## Biblical Saints

In the first century, the followers of Jesus did not have a formal system for
awarding titles. The New Testament writers used the word "saints" as a common
descriptor for all members of the faith community. For example, the Apostle Paul
addressed his letters to the "saints" in various cities. He wrote to "all those
in Rome who are loved by God and called to be saints" (Romans 1:7 (ESV)). He
also addressed the believers in Corinth as those "sanctified in Christ Jesus,
called to be saints" (1 Corinthians 1:2 (ESV)).

During this era, the term did not imply a special rank. It served as a reminder
of the common identity shared by all believers. The formal process of
canonization did not exist yet. Local communities recognized their own members
as being set apart for God's purposes.

## The Meaning of Hagios

The word translated as "saint" in the New Testament is the Greek adjective
*hagios*. This word carries a specific meaning that differs from modern
honorific titles.

The core meaning of *hagios* is "different" or "other." In a biblical context,
it refers to things or people that are set apart from the world because they
belong to the LORD. This application was not limited to a few elite
individuals. Every person who followed Christ was considered *hagios* because
they were dedicated to God's service. The Bible states that believers are "a
chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for his own
possession" (1 Peter 2:9 (ESV)).

## History of Canonization

The practice of official canonization developed several centuries after the
apostolic age. In the early church, the veneration of martyrs began at a local
level. Local bishops would approve the celebration of a particular individual's
life.

The first instance of a pope officially canonizing a saint occurred in 993 AD.
Pope John XV declared Ulric of Augsburg to be a saint after his death. This
marked a shift toward a more centralized process. In 1234 AD, Pope Gregory IX
reserved the right to canonize saints exclusively to the Holy See. This
established the formal legal procedure that many recognize today.

Critics might argue that a formal process is necessary to maintain order and
prevent the veneration of unworthy individuals. They suggest that official
recognition ensures that only those with proven "heroic virtue" receive the
title. While this provides a structured system for the church, it differs from
the biblical view where every believer is already "called to be a saint" by God.

## Conclusion

The biblical definition of a [saint](#biblical-saints) refers to every believer
who is [set apart](#the-meaning-of-hagios) for God. While modern
[dictionary definitions](#the-dictionary-definition) focus on official titles,
the original Greek word *hagios* emphasizes a shared identity in Christ. The
[history of canonization](#history-of-canonization) shows that the practice of
awarding titles was a later development in church history.

1. A [saint](#biblical-saints) is a person called by God to be holy.
2. The term [hagios](#the-meaning-of-hagios) means to be set apart or different
   from the world.
3. Official [canonization](#history-of-canonization) began as a formal church
   process in the 10th century.
