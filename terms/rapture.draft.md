# Rapture

What happens when Christ returns to the earth? Many Christians anticipate a
secret event where believers are suddenly removed from the world before a time
of great trouble. This concept, often called the rapture, has shaped modern
eschatology and influenced countless books and films.

## Theory Origins

In 1827, John Nelson Darby argued that the Bible taught a pretribulational
rapture. He convinced many other Christians of this view. Darby's leadership and
teachings attracted a growing number of followers. The movement spread to
England and other parts of the world.

Many years later, Hal Lindsey made the rapture theology popular. He was an
American author and Christian theologian. He published a book in 1970 titled
"The Late Great Planet Earth" that stated:

> The Bible teaches that there is coming a time when Jesus Christ will return
> for His church. But this will not be His final return to earth. This will be
> **a secret, invisible return in the air to meet those who believe in Him**. It
> will be a moment of unbelievable joy and excitement for those who are ready.
> But for those who are not ready, it will be a moment of terrible shock and
> sorrow. The Bible calls this event the 'rapture' which will take place at a
> time unknown to anyone except God the Father. — Hal Lindsey, The Late Great
> Planet Earth, Chapter 12

Pre-tribulationists often interpret biblical passages like 1 Thessalonians
4:13-17 and 1 Corinthians 15:51-52 to suggest two separate comings of Christ.
They see the rapture as a secret, pre-tribulation event to gather believers. A
later, visible second coming follows to establish his reign on Earth.

## Gathering the Elect

One of the verses quoted to prove the rapture is Matthew 24:31 (ESV).

> And he will send out his angels with a loud trumpet call, and they will gather
> his elect from the four winds, from one end of heaven to the other. — Matthew
> 24:31 (ESV)

Some arguments suggest this verse was a later addition to the scripture.
However, textual scholarship confirms that Matthew 24:31 is present in major
early manuscripts like Codex Sinaiticus and Codex Vaticanus.

Even with its authenticity established, the timing of the event is significant.
The angels gather the elect after the tribulation and the return of the Son of
Man. This sequence challenges the theory that the rapture occurs before the
tribulation.

## Noah's Days

> Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will not pass away. But
> concerning that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels of heaven, nor
> the Son, but the Father only. For as were the days of Noah, so will be the
> coming of the Son of Man. For as in those days before the flood they were
> eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day when Noah
> entered the ark, and they were unaware until the flood came and swept them all
> away, so will be the coming of the Son of Man. Then two men will be in the
> field; one will be taken and one left. Two women will be grinding at the mill;
> one will be taken and one left. Therefore, stay awake, for you do not know on
> what day your Lord is coming. But know this, that if the master of the house
> had known in what part of the night the thief was coming, he would have stayed
> awake and would not have let his house be broken into. Therefore you also must
> be ready, for the Son of Man is coming at an hour you do not expect. — Matthew
> 24:35-44 (ESV)

Jesus used the days of Noah to illustrate his return. Noah did not escape the
flood in the way rapture theories often suggest. Instead, the flood took those
who were unaware. Jesus echoed that his coming would be unexpected.

Some people believe the phrase "one will be taken and one left" refers to an
unexpected removal from Earth. The Greek word for "taken" is *paralambano*. It
means to receive near or take to oneself. It implies acceptance. For example,
Joseph took Mary as his wife in Matthew 1:20,24 (ESV).

The Greek word for "left" is *aphiemi*. It means to send away or leave alone.
This word is often associated with rejection or being left behind. Matthew 6:15
(ESV) uses this word in the context of forgiveness.

These terms suggest that two people may appear equal from a human perspective.
One is accepted like a bride. The other is rejected.

## Parables of Readiness

The theme of readiness appears in the parables following this passage.

### Faithful Servant

The faithful servant remains diligent while the master is away. The master sets
the faithful servant over all possessions upon his return. The wicked servant
assumes a delay and mistreats others. The master returns unexpectedly and
punishes the hypocrite.

> Who then is the faithful and wise servant, whom his master has set over his
> household, to give them their food at the proper time? Blessed is that servant
> whom his master will find so doing when he comes. Truly, I say to you, he will
> set him over all his possessions. — Matthew 24:45-47 (ESV)

### Ten Virgins

Ten virgins waited for a bridegroom. Five were wise and brought extra oil. Five
were foolish and did not. The bridegroom was delayed. When he arrived at
midnight, the wise virgins were ready and entered the marriage feast. The
foolish virgins were excluded.

> Watch therefore, for you know neither the day nor the hour. — Matthew 25:13
> (ESV)

### The Talents

A man entrusted his property to three servants. Two servants invested their
talents and doubled them. The master rewarded them. The third servant hid his
talent out of fear. The master rejected the worthless servant.

> For to everyone who has will more be given, and he will have an abundance. But
> from the one who has not, even what he has will be taken away. — Matthew 25:29
> (ESV)

## Defense against Critiques

Critics may argue that the term "taken" in Matthew 24:39 refers to those swept
away by the flood. In that verse, the Greek word is *airen*, which means to
carry away or remove. However, in verses 40 and 41, the word changes to
*paralambano*. This shift in vocabulary suggests a distinction between those
destroyed by judgment and those received by the Son of Man.
