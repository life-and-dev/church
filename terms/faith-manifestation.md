# Faith Manifestation

Some Christians confuse regular [“faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith) (trust) with the “manifestation of faith” (trust) or "gift of faith" (1 Corinthians 12:7-9) and think that the great miracles and healings in [the Bible](https://word.ofgod.info/downloads) were done simply because the person who did them had great “faith.”

## Trust

"Trust" is translated as "faith" in the New Testament and according to the dictionary it means:

> [assured](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/assured#h1) **reliance** on the character, ability, strength, or truth of someone or something
>
> — [Merriam-Webster Dictionary](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/trust)

* Normal "faith" (trust) can only occur if there is a trustworthy object to trust.
* "Manifestation of faith" (trust) occurs when God gives a specific revelation to someone to do something.

> Let’s say you have a friend whose car is being fixed, so you offer to give him a ride to work. He trusts you will actually show up and give him the ride, so he accepts. Then, because you are trustworthy, you keep your promise and give him the ride. But did his trust in you somehow force you give him the ride? Of course not. His trust gave him the confidence to be ready to be picked up at an appointed time, but it was your power that supplied the ride. Furthermore, you did not have to offer him the ride in the first place even though he was a trusting friend. At no point did the fact that he trusted you force you to act on his behalf.
>
> Biblical trust works the same way. We trust that God can heal, but **that does not force Him to heal**. Furthermore, **our trust does not give us the power to heal without God**. The fact that our trust in God does not “make” things happen explains why “faith” seems to fail so often. However, when God does tell us to heal — by giving us the **revelation to do it — then we can trust His “Yes” and command a healing**.
>
> -- [Spirit & Truth](https://www.revisedenglishversion.com/Appendix/2/Faith_is_Trust)

## The Power of Faith

Sometimes people receive what they want **because God chooses** to give grace, **not because faith itself has produced the result**. God acts according to His own purpose (Psalm 115:3; Isaiah 46:10), and His mercy does not depend on human desire or effort (Romans 9:16).

Elizabeth is an example. She became pregnant because God acted for His purpose and showed her grace in connection with John’s birth (Luke 1:5-25). So when people do not repeatedly receive what they want while trying hard to have [“faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith), it suggests the result comes from God’s love and mercy rather than from faith as a power in itself.

### Prayers

Sometimes when people are trying to have [“faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith) for something, they often **ask God for it repeatedly**. Yet Jesus taught persistent prayer as a means by which God answers requests (Luke 11:5-10; 18:1-8). So when someone keeps asking and later receives what he wants, the biblical explanation is that **God answered prayer, not that “faith” itself produced the result**.

This also explains why “faith” seems to fail so often or work only after long delays. If faith automatically produced results, people should receive what they believe for more consistently and more quickly. Instead, answers are often slow or absent. That fits the Bible’s teaching that outcomes depend on God’s timing and will, not on the strength of human belief.

Scripture also gives reasons prayers are not answered immediately or at all:

* Motives may be wrong (James 4:3)
* Spiritual conflict may cause delay (Daniel 10:10-13)
* Life in a fallen world still includes sowing and reaping (Hebrews 9:27; Galatians 6:7). 
  
Together, these passages show that results come from God’s decision to answer prayer, not from “faith” as a spiritual force.

### Positive attitude

[“Faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith) can seem effective because it often teaches people to **speak and act with confidence**. That alone can improve outcomes. People with **hope and purpose** often work harder, persevere longer, and cope better than people ruled by fear or despair.

But that does not prove that “faith” is a spiritual force or law. A positive attitude can influence effort, mood, and health, yet it **cannot guarantee** money, healing, or changed circumstances. Many cheerful, trusting people still suffer serious mental, physical, or material hardship.

Scripture also shows that a person’s **mindset affects the body** (Proverbs 3:7,8; 4:20-22; 12:4; 14:30; 15:13; 16:24; 17:22; 18:14). Rest, care, peace, prayer, and encouragement often help sick people recover. The Bible says, “A merry heart doeth good like a medicine” (Proverbs 17:22). That supports the power of emotional and spiritual wellbeing, not the idea that “faith” itself is a force that obtains results.

### Gift ministries

Sometimes [“faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith) seems to work because **God honors a person’s gift ministry, not because faith is a force**. Scripture says God gives different ministries and gifts, including healings and miracles (Romans 12:6-8; 1 Corinthians 12:28). So when someone sees repeated results in one area, he may **wrongly credit his personal faith** for what God is doing through that gift.

This also explains cases where healing happens without clear revelation. God may still act through [prayer](#prayers), humility, grace, or a gift ministry He has already given.

### Demonic influence

> An important reason why people believe there is a law that allows them to control things and bring success to themselves is that the Devil wants people to think they can control their lives and destiny without God. Sometimes when [“faith”](https://word.ofgod.info/terms/faith) or mental power, seems to work in helping people get what they want, it is because the Devil maneuvers situations and circumstances to make it seem like people are receiving things they have “faith” for. The Devil is constantly seeking to lead people away from God and truth, so he will sometimes help people acquire material things if it accomplishes his greater goal.
>
> A good explanation of how the Devil helps people was written many decades ago in the book, Think and Grow Rich, by Napoleon Hill. Hill is still considered a guru of financial self-help, and in his book, as the title suggests, he gives principles of the mind that supposedly allow people to acquire wealth. However, by the end of the book, he reveals the real secret to his success: getting help from a demon. Of course, Hill does not come out and say his helper is a demon, and he might not have fully understood it himself, although he did know **he was getting outside help from the spirit realm and not just from the power of his mind**.
>
> ...
>
> The Devil sometimes helps people gain worldly success explains why there are non-Christian groups that believe that the power of the mind taps into a “law” that works for both believers and unbelievers. In fact, when the “law” of faith is examined, there is really not much difference between what Word of Faith teachers say about [the power of “faith”](#the-power-of-faith) and what non-believers say about ***the power of the mind***.
>
> The book, The Secret, by Rhonda Byrne, which came out in 2006 and received great publicity, speaks of the “Secret” being the “law of attraction” (p. 25). Byrne, who says the “law of attraction” works for anybody, writes: 
> 
>> “The Secret gives you anything you want; happiness, health, and wealth” (p. 1). “If you can think about what you want in your mind, and make that your dominant thought, you will bring it into your life” (p. 9). “Nothing can come into your life unless you summon it through persistent thoughts” (p. 43). “The law of attraction is a law of nature. It is as impartial as the law of gravity” (p. 43).
>
> Byrne and others who believe in the power of the mind do not include God in their teaching, but still say the principles they operate are in the New Testament. Byrne writes: 
> 
>> “The Creative Process used in The Secret, which was taken from the New Testament in the Bible, is an easy guideline for you to create what you want in three simple steps” (The Secret, p. 47). 
>
> The steps she then outlines are: ask, believe, and receive.
>
> So in the final analysis, Word of Faith Christian ministers and power of the mind unbelievers **both believe in a law based on the power of the mind that gets people what they want**. Sometimes this law seems to work, often it does not. However, when it seems to work, it does not mean that there really is such a “law” or that the mind is somehow affecting the physical world.
>
> -- [Spirit & Truth](https://www.revisedenglishversion.com/Appendix/2/Faith_is_Trust)

## Conclusion

[Trust](#trust) in God should not be confused with a special manifestation of faith, and that [results come from God's action rather than from faith as a force](#the-power-of-faith). It also shows that apparent success may be explained through [answered prayer](#prayers), a [positive attitude](#positive-attitude), or [gift ministries](#gift-ministries), while [demonic influence](#demonic-influence) is presented as another reason some people wrongly conclude that faith operates like a spiritual law.
