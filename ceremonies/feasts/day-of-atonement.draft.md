6. Day of atonement (Yom Kippur)
   "the tenth day of this seventh month there shall be a day of atonement: it shall be a holy convocation to you; and you shall afflict your souls, and offer an offering made by fire to the LORD" (Lev 16:29-34, Lev 23:27)
   Kepts on 10th day of 7th month. (Lev 16:29)  It starts on the evening of the 9th day and ends at the evening of the 10th day (evening to evening). (Lev 23:32)
   You shall afflict your soul/humble yourself/weaken your bodies. (Lev 16:29; 23:27)
   "This shall be for you a law forever, to make atonement for the sons of Israel, for all their sins, once a year." (Lev 16:29,31,34; Lev 23:31-32)
   For on this day the anointed priest shall atone for you with holy linen garments, to cleanse you from all your sins you shall be clean before YAHWEH. (Lev 16:30-33)
   You shall do no work.  It is a Sabbath of rest to you, and you shall afflict/humble yourself. (Lev 16:29,31; 23:28; Lev 23:30-31)
   It shall be a holy gathering. (Lev 23:27)
   Any person who is not **humble/afflicted** in this same day, shall be cut off from his people. (Lev 23:29)
   Any person who does any work in this same day, shall be destroyed/cut off from his people (depending on translation). (Lev 23:30-32)

Meaning of humble / afflicted:

Original Word: עָנָה
Part of Speech: Verb
Transliteration: anah
Phonetic Spelling: (aw-naw')
Definition: to be bowed down or afflicted

fasting could be part of the "humble / afflicted" but if the focus is on the fasting and not on the affliction then it is wrong
