# The History of the Church

The history of the church is a complex tapestry woven from diverse cultural and
political influences. This article explores the pivotal events that shifted
religious practices and established the foundations of modern ecclesiastical
structures. By examining the transition from early Jewish traditions to the
institutionalization of the church, one can better understand the evolution of
its core beliefs and rituals.

## Jewish Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Before the Messiah | [Jewish Era](evolution/-1445-jewish-era.md) | The removal of the name of God. This tradition replaced the Tetragrammaton with titles like Adonai. |
| At the Messiah | [Jesus](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md) | The fulfillment of the messianic prophecies. This period included his [teachings](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md#teachings-of-jesus) (Matthew 22:37-40 (ESV)) and his [crucifixion](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md#trial-and-crucifixion). |
| After the Messiah | [Messianic Era](evolution/1st-c-messianic.md) | The persecution of followers and the spreading of the Gospel. |

## Christian Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1st century | [First Christians](evolution/1st-c-christians.md) | The adoption of the name "Christian". |
| 1st century | [Roman Imperial Cult](evolution/1st-c-roman-imperial-cult.md) | Intense persecution. The calendar was changed to honour Greek and Roman gods. |
| 1st century | [Gnosis](evolution/1st-c-gnosis.md) | Various Gnostic wisdom that influenced Christianity. |
| 156 AD | [Montanism](evolution/156-montanism.md) | The encouragement of authoritative church roles and the canonisation of the Bible. |
| 206 AD | [Tertullian](evolution/206-tertullian.md) | The invention of Trinity theology. This era saw the introduction of apostolic succession and the Eucharist. |
| 235 AD | [The Crisis of the Third Century](evolution/235-crisis-of-3rd-century.md) | The fall of emperors who were considered divine. |
| 312 AD | [Emperor Constantine](evolution/312-constantine.md) | The Roman Catholic Church was institutionalised. The sun god, Sol Invictus, was considered "Jesus Christ". |
| 325 AD | [The first Nicaea Creed](evolution/325-nicaea-creed.md) | Church authority was supported by the government. Christians were obliged to confess the divinity of Jesus. Jewish feasts were replaced with Christian feasts. Christianity became mixed with paganism. |
| 337 AD | Constantius II and Bishop Ulfilas | Constantius II relaxed the church creeds. This allowed Bishop Ulfilas to [spread Arianism](evolution/337-arianism.md) in the Gothic regions. |
| 363 AD | The Council of Laodicea | [Sunday Rest was instituted](terms/sundays.md). Those who rested on a Sabbath were persecuted. |
| 379 AD | Theodosius I and the Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed | [Nicene Christianity became a state religion](evolution/380-state-religion.md). Other heresies like Arianism were criminalised. |
| 382 AD | St. Jerome | The translation of the first Latin Christian bible, known as [the Vulgate Bible](evolution/382-vulgate.md). This was done by a supporter of Nicene orthodoxy. |
| 395 AD | The Dark Ages | [All Arian nations fall](evolution/395-dark-ages.md) in various wars. Nicene Christianity survived these conflicts. |
| 494 AD | Pope Gelasius | The institution of [Valentine's Day](evolution/494-valentine.md). |
| 731 AD | Pope Gregory III | The institution of [All Saint's Day](evolution/609-halloween.md). This later evolved into Halloween. |
| 800 AD | [Holy Roman Empire](evolution/800-holy-roman-empire.md) | Pope Stephen II formed alliances that strengthened papal involvement in politics. Papal influence spread across Europe. Any religious practices against the Roman Catholic Church were suppressed. |
| 1074 AD | Pope Gregory VII | The [prohibition of clerical marriages](evolution/1074-clerical-marriages.md). |
| 1095 AD | [The Crusades](evolution/1095-crusades.md) | Various Christian crusades were attempted by the Byzantine Empire. They aimed to regain territory occupied by Muslims. Soldiers believed that salvation could be secured by participating in these wars. |
| 1215 AD | Pope Innocent III | The decree of [seven sacraments](evolution/1215-7-sacraments.md) as necessary for salvation. He declared that nobody can be saved outside the church. |
| 1229 AD | Council of Toulouse | The [prohibition of Bible ownership](evolution/bible-ownership.drafts.md). |
| 1302 AD | Pope Boniface VIII | The declaration that [submission under the authority of the Pope is necessary for salvation](evolution/papal-authority.drafts.md). |

## Conclusion

The foundational period of the faith was defined by [Jewish Influences](#jewish-influences).
Subsequent institutional changes were driven by [Christian Influences](#christian-influences).
