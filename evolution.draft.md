# The History of the Church

The Christian church has a rich and complex history, shaped by a variety of influences over the centuries. This article provides a brief overview of the key periods and figures that played a role in the evolution of the church, from its Jewish roots to its establishment as a major world religion.

## Jewish Influences

| Period             | Influencers                | Influence                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Before the Messiah | [Jewish Era](evolution/jewish.draft.md)       | Substitution of God's name (YHWH) with "the LORD" out of reverence (Exodus 20:7)                                                              |
| At the Messiah     | Jesus          | The fulfilment of the messianic prophecies, teachings, and the crucifixion |
| After the Messiah  | [Messianic Era](evolution/messianic.draft.md) | Persecution and spreading of the Gospel                                                                                                       |

## Christian Influences

| Period      | Influencers                                              | Influence                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1st century | [First Christians](evolution/christian.draft.md)                            | The adoption of the name "Christian" (Acts 11:26)                                                                                                                                                          |
| 1st century | [Roman Imperial Cult](evolution/roman-imperial-cult.draft.md)               | Persecution of Christians for refusing to worship the emperor                                                                                                                              |
| 1st century | [Gnosis](evolution/gnosis.draft.md)                                         | A rival theological movement whose claims to secret knowledge (gnosis) prompted early Christian authors to define orthodoxy                                                                              |
| 156 AD      | [Montanism](evolution/montanism.draft.md)                                   | A prophetic movement that challenged church authority with claims of new revelations from the Holy Spirit                                                                                                |
| 206 AD      | [Tertullian](evolution/tertullian.draft.md)                                 | The first Latin theologian to use the term "Trinity" and a key figure in the development of early Church doctrine on topics like apostolic succession and the Eucharist                          |
| 235 AD      | [The Crisis of the Third Century](evolution/crisis-of-3rd-century.draft.md) | The fall of "divine" emperors                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 312 AD      | [Emperor Constantine](evolution/constantine.draft.md)                       | Paved the way for the institutionalization of the Roman Catholic Church and Christian art adopted imagery from the Sol Invictus cult, depicting Jesus as the sun god                                |
| 325 AD      | [The first Nicaea Creed](evolution/325-nicaea-creed.md)               | Solidified the alliance between church and state, established the divinity of Jesus as official doctrine, and replaced the observance of Passover with Easter                                    |
| 337 AD      | Constantius II and Bishop Ulfilas                        | Constantius II relaxed the church's creeds which allowed Bishop Ulfilas to [spread Arianism](evolution/arianism.draft.md) in the Gothic regions                                                                               |
| 363 AD      | The Council of Laodicea                                  | [Sunday Rest was instituted](evolution/363-outlawing-sabbath.md) (Canon 29) and those who continued to observe the Sabbath were condemned                                                                                             |
| 379 AD      | Theodosius I and the Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed     | [Nicene Christianity became a state religion](evolution/380-state-religion.md) and other "heresies" like Arianism was criminalised                                                                                          |
| 382 AD      | St. Jerome                                               | The first Latin Christian bible, namely [the Vulgate Bible](evolution/vulgate.draft.md), was translated by a supporter of Nicene orthodoxy                                                                                    |
| 395 AD      | The Dark Ages                                            | Germanic tribes convert to Arianism, but Nicene Christianity eventually prevails as Arian kingdoms fall or convert                                                                                                     |
| 494 AD      | Pope Gelasius                                            | Established the Feast of Saint Valentine, which may have been an attempt to replace the pagan festival of Lupercalia                                                                                             |
| 731 AD      | Pope Gregory III                                         | Instituted [All Saint's Day](evolution/all-saints.draft.md) which evolved later into Halloween                                                                                                                                |
| 800 AD      | [Holy Roman Empire](evolution/holy-roman-empire.draft.md)                   | Pope Stephen II formed alliances which strengthen the papal involvement in politics<br>Papal influence spread across Europe<br>Suppress any religious practises against the Roman Catholic Church          |
| 1074 AD     | Pope Gregory VII                                         | [Prohibition of clerical marriages](evolution/clerical-marriages.draft.md)                                                                                                                                                    |
| 1095 AD     | [The Crusades](evolution/crusades.draft.md)                                 | Various Christian crusades were attempted by the Byzantine Empire to regain territory occupied by Muslims<br>Soldiers believed that salvation could be secured by participation in these wars              |
| 1215 AD     | Pope Innocent III                                        | Decree [7 sacraments](evolution/1215-7-sacraments.md) necessary for salvation<br>Declared that nobody can be saved outside the church                                                                                        |
| 1229 AD     | Council of Toulouse                                      | [Prohibition of Bible ownership](evolution/bible-ownership.draft.md)                                                                                                                                                          |
| 1302 AD     | Pope Boniface VIII                                       | Declared [submission under the authority of the Pope is necessary for salvation](evolution/papal-authority.draft.md)                                                                                                          |

## Conclusion

The history of the church is a long and complex one, with many different factors shaping its development. From its early days as a Jewish sect to its current status as a major world religion, the church has been influenced by a wide range of people, events, and ideas. This article has provided a brief overview of some of the most important of these, but there is much more to learn.
