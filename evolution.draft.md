# The Historical Evolution of Church Traditions

How did the simple faith of the first-century disciples transform into the
complex institutionalized religion we see today? Throughout history, various
leaders, councils, and cultural shifts have left an indelible mark on church
practices and beliefs. This article examines the key events and influencers that
shaped the traditions of the church, beginning with its roots in Judaism and
tracing its development through centuries of change.

## Jewish Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Before the Messiah | [Jewish Era](evolution/-1445-jewish-era.md) | Removal of God's name |
| At the Messiah | [Jesus](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md) | The [fulfilment of the messianic prophecies](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md#teachings-of-jesus), [teachings](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md#teachings-of-jesus), and [the crucifixion](evolution/1st-c-jesus.md#trial-and-crucifixion) |
| After the Messiah | [Messianic Era](evolution/1st-c-messianic.md) | Persecution and spreading of the Gospel |

## Christian Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1st century | [First Christians](evolution/1st-c-christians.md) | The adoption of the name "Christian" |
| 1st century | [Roman Imperial Cult](evolution/1st-c-roman-imperial-cult.md) | More intense persecution, changing calendar to honour Greek and Roman gods |
| 1st century | [Gnosis](evolution/1st-c-gnosis.md) | Various Gnostic "wisdom" that influenced Christianity |
| 156 AD | [Montanism](evolution/156-montanism.md) | Encouraged authoritative church roles and the canonisation of the bible |
| 206 AD | [Tertullian](evolution/206-tertullian.md) | The invention of the Trinity theology, apostolic succession and Eucharist |
| 235 AD | [The Crisis of the Third Century](evolution/235-crisis-of-3rd-century.md) | The fall of "divine" emperors |
| 312 AD | [Emperor Constantine](evolution/312-constantine.md) | The Roman Catholic Church was institutionalised. The sun god, Sol Invictus, was considered "Jesus Christ" |
| 325 AD | [The first Nicaea Creed](evolution/325-nicaea-creed.md) | Church authority was supported by the government. Christians were obliged to confess Jesus' divinity. Jewish feasts were replaced with "Christian" feasts. Christianity became mixed with paganism |
| 337 AD | Constantius II and Bishop Ulfilas | Constantius II relaxed the church's creeds which allowed Bishop Ulfilas to [spread Arianism](evolution/337-arianism.md) in the Gothic regions |
| 363 AD | The Council of Laodicea | [Sunday Rest was instituted](terms/sundays.md) and those who rest on a Sabbath were persecuted |
| 379 AD | Theodosius I and the Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed | [Nicene Christianity became a state religion](evolution/380-state-religion.md) and other "heresies" like Arianism was criminalised |
| 382 AD | St. Jerome | The first Latin Christian bible, namely [the Vulgate Bible](evolution/382-vulgate.md), was translated by a supporter of Nicene orthodoxy |
| 395 AD | The Dark Ages | [All Arian nations fall](evolution/395-dark-ages.md) in various wars, but Nicene Christianity survives |
| 494 AD | Pope Gelasius | Instituted [Valentine's Day](evolution/494-valentine.md) |
| 731 AD | Pope Gregory III | Instituted [All Saint's Day](evolution/609-halloween.md) which evolved later into Halloween |
| 800 AD | [Holy Roman Empire](evolution/800-holy-roman-empire.md) | Pope Stephen II formed alliances which strengthen the papal involvement in politics. Papal influence spread across Europe. Suppress any religious practises against the Roman Catholic Church |
| 1074 AD | Pope Gregory VII | [Prohibition of clerical marriages](evolution/clerical-marriages.draft.md) |
| 1095 AD | [The Crusades](evolution/1095-crusades.md) | Various Christian crusades were attempted by the Byzantine Empire to regain territory occupied by Muslims. Soldiers believed that salvation could be secured by participation in these wars |
| 1215 AD | Pope Innocent III | Decree [7 sacraments](evolution/1215-7-sacraments.md) necessary for salvation. Declared that nobody can be saved outside the church |
| 1229 AD | Council of Toulouse | [Prohibition of Bible ownership](evolution/bible-ownership.drafts.md) |
| 1302 AD | Pope Boniface VIII | Declared [submission under the authority of the Pope is necessary for salvation](evolution/papal-authority.drafts.md) |

## Conclusion

The church has evolved through a combination of early [Jewish
Influences](#jewish-influences) and subsequent [Christian
Influences](#christian-influences). These historical shifts transformed the
faith from its [initial messianic roots](#jewish-influences) into a structured
[institutional religion](#christian-influences).
