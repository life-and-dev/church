# The Evolution of the Church

The history of the church is a complex journey that spans centuries, beginning
with its deep roots in Jewish tradition and the life of Jesus. As the movement
transitioned from a small group of disciples into a powerful global
institution, it was shaped by theological debates, political alliances, and
cultural shifts. This article explores the key periods and figures that
influenced the development of the church from the first century through the
Middle Ages.

## Jewish Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Before Messiah | [Jewish Era][1] | Removal of God's name |
| At Messiah | [Jesus][2] | [Messianic prophecies][2], [teachings][2], [crucifixion][2] |
| After Messiah | [Messianic Era][3] | Persecution and spreading of the Gospel |

## Christian Influences

| Period | Influencers | Influence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1st century | [First Christians][4] | The adoption of the name "Christian" |
| 1st century | [Roman Imperial Cult][5] | More intense persecution. |
| 1st century | [Gnosis][6] | Various Gnostic "wisdom" influenced Christianity. |
| 156 AD | [Montanism][7] | Encouraged church roles and [bible canonisation][25]. |
| 206 AD | [Tertullian][8] | Invention of Trinity, apostolic succession. |
| 235 AD | [Crisis of 3rd Century][9] | The fall of "divine" emperors. |
| 312 AD | [Emperor Constantine][10] | The church was institutionalised. |
| 313 AD | [Edict of Milan][26] | Established [religious tolerance][26]. |
| 325 AD | [First Nicaea Creed][11] | Church authority supported by the government. |
| 337 AD | Constantius II | Allowed Bishop Ulfilas to [spread Arianism][12]. |
| 363 AD | Council of Laodicea | [Sunday Rest was instituted][13]. |
| 379 AD | Theodosius I | [Nicene Christianity became a state religion][14]. |
| 382 AD | St. Jerome | The first Latin Christian bible: [Vulgate][15]. |
| 395 AD | The Dark Ages | [All Arian nations fall][16]. |
| 494 AD | Pope Gelasius | Instituted [Valentine's Day][17]. |
| 731 AD | Pope Gregory III | Instituted [All Saints' Day][18] on November 1. |
| 800 AD | [Holy Roman Empire][19] | Pope Stephen II formed political alliances. |
| 1074 AD | Pope Gregory VII | [Prohibition of clerical marriages][20]. |
| 1095 AD | [The Crusades][21] | Christian crusades attempted by Byzantine Empire. |
| 1215 AD | Pope Innocent III | Decree [7 sacraments][22] necessary for salvation. |
| 1229 AD | [Council of Toulouse][27] | [Prohibited laypeople from owning Bibles][27]. |
| 1302 AD | Pope Boniface VIII | [Submission to Pope necessary for salvation][24]. |

## Conclusion

The history of the church reflects a significant transition from its early
[Jewish Influences](#jewish-influences) to the widespread
[Christian Influences](#christian-influences) that institutionalized the faith.
Over the centuries, the church evolved from a persecuted sect into a dominant
political and religious power, profoundly impacting the course of Western
history.
