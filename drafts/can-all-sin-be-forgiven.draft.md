# Can All Sins Be Forgiven?

Many people carry the weight of their past and wonder if they have crossed a
line that God will not forgive. The concept of an "unforgivable sin" can cause
great anxiety and fear for those seeking spiritual peace. This article explores
the biblical perspective on forgiveness, the nature of the Holy Spirit's work,
and the hope available to everyone.

## The Unforgivable Sin

The only sin described as unforgivable is blasphemy against the Holy Spirit.
Jesus explained that while every other sin and blasphemy can be forgiven,
speaking against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven in this age or the age to
come. This is because the Holy Spirit is the one who convicts people of their
sin and leads them to repentance. If a person deliberately rejects the work of
the Spirit, they lose the only means by which they can be brought to
forgiveness.

> Therefore I tell you, every sin and blasphemy will be forgiven people, but
> the blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven. And whoever speaks a
> word against the Son of Man will be forgiven, but whoever speaks against the
> Holy Spirit will not be forgiven, either in this age or in the age to come. —
> Matthew 12:31-32 (ESV)

## Conviction and Repentance

The Holy Spirit plays a vital role by bringing awareness of sin. Without this
conviction, a person would not see their need for God's grace.

> And when he comes, he will convict the world concerning sin and righteousness
> and judgment: — John 16:8 (ESV)

David Mathis notes that if a person worries about having committed the
unforgivable sin, it is a strong sign that they have not done so. Hearts that
are completely hardened against the Lord and His Spirit do not worry about their
spiritual state.

> If you worry about unforgivable sin, then most likely you are not there. Not
> yet. Hearts with settled hardness against Jesus and his Spirit don't go around
> worrying about it. — [David Mathis
> (desiringGod)](https://www.desiringgod.org/articles/what-is-the-unforgivable-sin#hope-for-those-feeling-unforgivable)

## Stubbornness of Heart

In the Old Testament, the LORD warned about the dangers of a stubborn heart
that knowingly turns away from His covenant. Moses described a person who hears
the words of the covenant but continues to walk in their own way, believing they
will be safe.

> Beware lest there be among you a man or woman or clan or tribe whose heart is
> turning away today from the LORD our God to go and serve the gods of those
> nations. Beware lest there be among you a root bearing poisonous and bitter
> fruit, one who, when he hears the words of this sworn covenant, blesses
> himself in his heart, saying, 'I shall be safe, though I walk in the
> stubbornness of my heart.' This will lead to the sweeping away of moist and
> dry alike. The LORD will not be willing to forgive him, but rather the anger
> of the LORD and his jealousy will smoke against that man, and the curses
> written in this book will settle upon him, and the LORD will blot out his name
> from under heaven. And the LORD will single him out from all the tribes of
> Israel for calamity, in accordance with all the curses of the covenant written
> in this Book of the Law. — Deuteronomy 29:18-21 (ESV)

Stubbornness in this context refers to a deliberate and rebellious choice to
disobey the LORD. It is different from being deceived or making accidental
mistakes.

## Examples of Forgiveness

The Bible provides many examples of individuals who committed serious sins but
were still forgiven. These accounts demonstrate the depth of mercy available.

### Blasphemy and Grace

Jesus stated that all sins will be forgiven, including whatever blasphemies
people utter. Even those who spoke against the Son of Man could find grace.

> Truly, I say to you, all sins will be forgiven the children of man, and
> whatever blasphemies they utter, — Mark 3:28 (ESV)

### Broken Vows

Under the Law, the priests could make atonement for those who realized their
guilt after making rash oaths or vows. If the high priests could facilitate
freedom for these people, Jesus Christ is certainly qualified to do the same.

> or if anyone utters with his lips a rash oath to do evil or to do good, any
> sort of rash oath that people swear... when he comes to know it, and he
> realizes his guilt in any of these; when he realizes his guilt in any of these
> and confesses the sin he has committed... And the priest shall make atonement
> for him for the sin that he has committed, and he shall be forgiven. —
> Leviticus 5:4-5,10 (ESV)

### Public Denial

Peter denied the Lord publicly three times. However, the Lord showed him grace
and restored him to a position of leadership.

> When they had finished breakfast, Jesus said to Simon Peter, 'Simon, son of
> John, do you love me more than these?' He said to him, 'Yes, Lord; you know
> that I love you.' He said to him, 'Feed my lambs.' — John 21:15 (ESV)

### Murder and Adultery

King David committed adultery and arranged the death of a man. When he
confessed his sin, the LORD put away his sin and spared his life. His prayer of
repentance remains a powerful testimony of God's ability to cleanse even the
deepest bloodguiltiness.

> David said to Nathan, 'I have sinned against the LORD.' And Nathan said to
> David, 'The LORD also has put away your sin; you shall not die.' — 2 Samuel
> 12:13 (ESV)
>
> Deliver me from bloodguiltiness, O God, O God of my salvation, and my tongue
> will sing aloud of your righteousness. — Psalm 51:14 (ESV)

### Prostitution and Criminality

Jesus taught that prostitutes and tax collectors were entering the kingdom of
God because of their repentance. Even a criminal hanging on a cross was promised
paradise when he turned to the Lord.

> Jesus said to them, 'Truly, I say to you, the tax collectors and the
> prostitutes go into the kingdom of God before you.' — Matthew 21:31 (ESV)
>
> And he said, 'Jesus, remember me when you come into your kingdom.' And he
> said to him, 'Truly, I say to you, today you will be with me in paradise.' —
> Luke 23:42-43 (ESV)

### Witchcraft and Persecution

Simon the sorcerer believed and was baptized after seeing the power of God.
Saul, who actively persecuted the church and dragged believers to prison, also
received grace and became a chosen instrument of the Lord.

> Even Simon himself believed, and after being baptized he continued with
> Philip. And seeing signs and great miracles performed, he was amazed. — Acts
> 8:13 (ESV)
>
> But Saul, still breathing threats and murder against the disciples of the
> Lord, went to the high priest... Now as he went on his way, he approached
> Damascus, and suddenly a light from heaven shone around him. — Acts 9:1,3
> (ESV)

## The Promise of Cleansing

The message of the gospel is one of hope and restoration. If a person confesses
their sins, God is faithful to forgive and cleanse them from all
unrighteousness.

> If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to
> cleanse us from all unrighteousness. — 1 John 1:9 (ESV)

This promise applies to anyone who recognizes their need for mercy and turns to
the Lord. He has the power to set people free from their sins and their
circumstances.

## Defense Against Criticism

Critics may argue that the existence of an "unforgivable sin" contradicts the
idea of an all-loving God. However, the limitation is not on God's willingness
to forgive. It is on the human heart's willingness to repent. Forgiveness
requires a recipient who is open to receiving it. If the Holy Spirit is the
agent of repentance and His work is permanently rejected, the person remains in
a state where they cannot be forgiven because they will not seek it. This is a
logical necessity of free will and the nature of restoration.

## Conclusion

The only sin that remains unforgivable is the permanent rejection of the Holy
Spirit's convicting work. [The Unforgivable Sin](#the-unforgivable-sin). Feeling
conviction for one's actions is evidence that the Holy Spirit is still working
and has not abandoned the individual. [Conviction and
Repentance](#conviction-and-repentance). The Bible provides many examples of
individuals who committed grave sins but found complete forgiveness through
faith and confession. [Examples of Forgiveness](#examples-of-forgiveness). Full
restoration and cleansing are promised to everyone who turns to the Lord in
repentance. [The Promise of Cleansing](#the-promise-of-cleansing).
